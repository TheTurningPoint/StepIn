Help & how-to

Quick guides for residents, house managers, and owners. Stuck on something not covered here? Email hello@instepapp.com.

For residents

Signing in

Open your house's link and enter your name and 4-digit PIN. Forgot it? Tap Forgot PIN? on the sign-in screen — if you have an email on file you can reset it yourself; otherwise ask your house manager.

Curfew — signing in and out

From Home, tap I'm leaving and say where you're going, then I'm home when you're back. Your phone's location is recorded at check-in.

Meeting check-ins

Tap Check in to a meeting, pick the meeting, and have someone there sign as your witness. It counts toward your weekly requirement.

Documents & concerns

Sign house documents from your Home screen when they appear. To raise an issue, use File a concern — you can do it anonymously.

For house managers

Adding residents

Actions → Add resident. Set their name, a temporary PIN, and contact/emergency info. Required documents send to them automatically.

Screenings & incidents

Actions → Log a screening (urine, breathalyzer, or both — each gets its own result) or an incident. Both capture signatures and timestamps.

Reports & exports

The Report tab covers attendance, screenings, incidents, and grievances. Each prints to a clean PDF or exports to CSV for your NARR file. The Home and In/Out cards also export to CSV.

Resetting a resident's PIN

Manage resident → pick the resident → Reset PIN. They sign in with the new one and can change it.

For owners

Multiple houses

The owner view rolls up every house. Tap a house to drill in. Each house's manager only sees their own house.

Settings & branding

Settings → Organization & managers: set your org name (shown on the app, reports, and emails), the weekly meeting requirement, lab policy, and each house's check-in location.

Reminders & weekly reports

Residents who opt in get daily nudges about documents and announcements. Owners/managers with an email get a weekly summary every Monday. Set your email in Settings → Change my PIN → My email.

Activity log

Settings → Activity log shows who changed or removed records — useful for audits and questions.

How your data is protected

Recovery records are sensitive, so InStep is built so only the right people can see them:

  • Login-gated — nothing is visible without signing in; there's no public page that exposes anyone's information.
  • Walled off per house & organization — each house's data is isolated from every other house and organization, enforced by the database itself.
  • PINs are scrambled — stored as one-way hashes, never as plain text.
  • Encrypted in transit — everything travels over HTTPS, the same encryption a bank's website uses.
  • Activity log — owners can see who changed or removed any record, and when (Settings → Activity log).
  • Your data is yours — export records any time, and have them deleted on request.

Full details are in the Privacy Policy & Terms. Questions about data: hello@instepapp.com.
