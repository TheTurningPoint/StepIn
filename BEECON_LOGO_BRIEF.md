# Beecon — Logo Mark Commission Brief

*Prompts to hand a designer, or paste into an image generator. The wordmark ("beecon" — lowercase
serif, dark ink, gold four-point catchlight star between the e's) is locked; this is for the standalone
**mark** that pairs with it and stands alone as favicon / app icon / avatar.*

---

## Master brief (inherit into every prompt below)

> **Beecon** is a software studio whose products guide people home — the name folds **bee + beacon +
> light**, in memory of the founder's late fiancée, Bee. The mark must **emit light, not watch**: a
> source, never a sensor. Warm like a candle, porch light, or hearth — never clinical, never
> surveillance.
>
> **Palette:** gold **#E8B24A → #DFA23C**, warm dark ink **#241C15**, cream **#F5F1EA**. The gold is
> non-negotiable — it carries forward from the four-point catchlight star in the existing wordmark.
>
> **Lockup:** the mark sits LEFT of the locked lowercase serif wordmark "beecon" (dark-brown ink, small
> gold four-point star nestled between the two e's). It must also stand alone as favicon/app-icon/avatar.
>
> **Technical:** legible at 16px; works in one color; works on light AND dark; vector/SVG-first; no
> gradient-dependent legibility; simple beats detailed.
>
> **Never:** a generic AI four-point sparkle, a flat crypto/dev hexagon badge, a lighthouse-postcard
> tower, a literal cartoon bee, or anything eye-like.
>
> **The test:** could it sit lit on a porch railing?
>
> **Deliverables:** SVG master; favicon 16/32; app icon 512 + maskable; one-color versions on light and
> dark; mark alone; mark-left-of-wordmark lockup.

---

## Five directions, best first

### 1 — "The Kept Light" (hexagon lantern)
Fuses both halves of the name into one object: a honeycomb cell that has become a lantern — a cell that
holds light instead of honey. **Bee lives in it** as the hexagon (quietly); the gold star inside is the
same star from between the e's, now kept safe inside glass.

**(A) Human-designer brief:** Design a single hexagonal cell, softly rounded at the corners so it reads
as lantern glass rather than a tech badge — vertical orientation (point-up or flat-top, explore both),
stroke-drawn in warm dark ink #241C15. Inside, centered, one gold four-point star (#E8B24A) matching the
catchlight star in the beecon wordmark — the hex is the housing, the star is the flame. Consider a subtle
warmth cue at the top edge (a small loop or cap suggesting a hung lantern) only if it survives 16px. It
should feel like a kept light: something tended, carried, left burning in a window — not a crypto logo.

**(B) AI image-generator prompt:**
> Minimal flat vector logo mark: a single rounded-corner hexagon drawn as a thin warm dark-brown outline
> (#241C15), read as a small lantern or glass honeycomb cell, with one warm gold four-point star (#E8B24A)
> glowing at its center like a kept flame. Soft, warm, hand-tended feeling — a candle lantern on a porch,
> not a tech badge. Generous negative space inside the hexagon around the star. Flat vector, centered,
> single subject, plain cream background (#F5F1EA), no gradients, no texture, legible at favicon size.
> **Avoid:** eyes, photorealism, crypto or blockchain hexagon badges, sharp geometric tech logos,
> lighthouse towers, cartoon bees, honeycomb clusters, generic AI sparkle, letters or text, 3D, drop shadows.

### 2 — "The b That Holds the Light" (monogram)
A lowercase serif "b" whose bowl is a lit window — the initial of both the studio and of Bee, in the same
typographic voice as the locked wordmark. As a favicon it's unmistakably *beecon*. **Bee lives in it** as
her own initial, sheltering the gold star the way the wordmark's e's do.

**(A) Human-designer brief:** Draw a single lowercase serif "b" in warm dark ink #241C15, matching or
sympathetic to the beecon wordmark's serif. Open or hollow the bowl of the b so it reads as a small lit
window or lamp-glass, and place the gold four-point star (#E8B24A) inside it — the letter becomes a lamp
post holding its own light. The ascender should feel calm and bookish, not condensed or techy. Test the
one-color version early: at 16px the star may need to become a simple diamond or dot of gold — acceptable.

**(B) AI image-generator prompt:**
> Minimal flat vector logo mark: a single lowercase serif letter "b" in warm dark brown (#241C15), classic
> bookish serif, where the round bowl of the letter is hollow like a small lit window and holds one warm
> gold four-point star (#E8B24A) at its center, as if the letter itself is a lamp. Warm, literary, quiet —
> a reading lamp, not a font specimen. Flat vector, centered, single subject, plain cream background
> (#F5F1EA), no gradients, favicon-legible.
> **Avoid:** eyes, photorealism, sans-serif or techno letterforms, additional letters or words, crypto
> hexagons, lighthouse towers, cartoon bees, generic AI sparkle, 3D, gradients, ornament.
> *(For this one only, the letter "b" is intentional — override any "no letters" rule.)*

### 3 — "The Porch Light" (lit window)
The promise stated plainly — *somebody left the light on for you.* A small window, warm inside, is the
most universal "guides you home" symbol that isn't a lighthouse. **Bee lives in it** as the light in the
window: the same four-point catchlight, burning where someone is waited for.

**(A) Human-designer brief:** Design a simple arched or square window shape — a single pane or a soft
arch-top, stroke-drawn in #241C15 with slightly rounded corners so it feels domestic, not architectural.
The interior glows: fill it cream-to-gold or, in the one-color version, leave the pane open with the gold
four-point star (#E8B24A) centered in it. Optionally divide with one thin muntin cross ONLY if it survives
16px — otherwise the star alone carries it. It must feel like dusk seen from the road: warm inside, someone home.

**(B) AI image-generator prompt:**
> Minimal flat vector logo mark: a single small arch-top window shape with softly rounded corners, thin
> warm dark-brown outline (#241C15), glowing warmly from within, one gold four-point star (#E8B24A) centered
> inside the pane like a light left on for someone coming home at dusk. Domestic, warm, welcoming — a cottage
> window, not a building. Flat vector, centered, single subject, plain cream background (#F5F1EA), no
> gradients required for legibility, favicon-legible.
> **Avoid:** eyes, photorealism, full houses or buildings, lighthouse towers, church windows, crypto
> hexagons, cartoon bees, generic AI sparkle, letters or text, 3D, drop shadows, multiple windows.

### 4 — "The Sheltered Flame" (wings around the light)
Two soft curved forms — cupped hands, petals, or wings — protecting a light: the studio's ethic in one
gesture, the light *tended*, not just displayed. **Bee lives in it** as abstracted wings that hold the
light rather than being drawn.

**(A) Human-designer brief:** Compose two symmetrical curved strokes in #241C15 — soft, calligraphic,
leaf- or wing-like — rising and curving inward around a central gold four-point star (#E8B24A), like hands
cupped around a candle in wind. The wings must stay abstract: no insect body, no head, no antennae, no
more than two forms. Negative space between the curves and the star is what makes it breathe; at 16px the
whole mark should still read as "something small and bright, protected."

**(B) AI image-generator prompt:**
> Minimal flat vector logo mark: two soft symmetrical curved strokes in warm dark brown (#241C15), like
> abstract wings or gently cupped hands, rising on either side of and sheltering one warm gold four-point
> star (#E8B24A) at the center, as if protecting a candle flame from the wind. Tender, calm, protective —
> a tended flame, not a religious icon. Generous negative space. Flat vector, centered, single subject,
> plain cream background (#F5F1EA), no gradients, favicon-legible.
> **Avoid:** eyes, photorealism, literal bees or insects, insect bodies or antennae, angel wings, praying
> hands, laurel wreaths, crypto hexagons, lighthouse towers, generic AI sparkle, letters or text, 3D.

### 5 — "The Way Home" (guiding star over a path)
The beacon as wayfinding — her star doing its actual job, standing over the road that leads back. One
horizon line under one light turns "sparkle" into "north star." **Bee lives in it** as her star, unchanged
from the wordmark, here given the sky with one long ray reaching down to the road.

**(A) Human-designer brief:** Place one gold four-point star (#E8B24A) with a deliberately elongated lower
ray, descending toward a single short horizon or path stroke in #241C15 beneath it — light touching down
where someone walks. The elongated downward ray is what separates this from a generic sparkle: the light
*reaches*. Explore the star at wordmark-catchlight proportions vs. slightly softened points; the ground
stroke can curve faintly like a road. Two elements maximum; if the ground line muddies at 16px, the
long-rayed star must carry the mark alone.

**(B) AI image-generator prompt:**
> Minimal flat vector logo mark: one warm gold four-point star (#E8B24A) with a distinctly elongated lower
> ray reaching downward like a beam of light, touching a single short, gently curved warm dark-brown ground
> line (#241C15) beneath it — a north star lighting the path home at night. Only two elements: star and
> ground line. Quiet, hopeful, directional. Flat vector, centered, single subject, plain cream background
> (#F5F1EA), no gradients, favicon-legible.
> **Avoid:** eyes, photorealism, multiple stars, starbursts, generic symmetrical AI sparkle with equal
> rays, night skies, moons, mountains, lighthouse towers, crypto hexagons, cartoon bees, letters or text,
> 3D, glow effects.

## How to brief your designer & judge the returns

Hand them three things: the **beecon wordmark file** (it's locked — the mark must live to its left and
speak its language), the **exact hex values** (#E8B24A/#DFA23C gold, #241C15 ink, #F5F1EA cream — her gold
is the through-line, not a suggestion), and the **original eye logo as spirit reference only** — say
plainly: *this is the feeling we're keeping and the form we're retiring; the new mark emits light instead
of watching.* Ask for **16px one-color silhouettes first**, before any polish — a sheet of tiny dark
shapes on cream and gold shapes on ink — because a mark that fails at favicon size fails everywhere, and
you'll know in five seconds which direction has bones. Then judge every submission against the single
question that already contains the palette, the warmth, the anti-references, and Bee: **does it look like
it could sit lit on a porch railing, waiting for someone to come home?**
