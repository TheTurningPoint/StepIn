Privacy Policy & Terms

Last updated: June 29, 2026

InStep is a management tool for sober living and recovery residences. This policy explains what information the app handles and how it's protected. Each recovery residence (the "operator") decides what to collect about its residents; InStep provides the software that stores and displays it.

This is not legal or medical advice. Recovery-related information can be sensitive and, depending on the operator and services involved, may be subject to laws such as 42 CFR Part 2 or HIPAA. Operators are responsible for their own legal compliance and for the consent of their residents.

What we collect

  • Resident profile: name, login PIN, house, recovery phase, move-in and sobriety dates, status.
  • Recovery activity: meeting check-ins (including approximate GPS location and a witness signature), curfew sign in/out, chores, and event participation.
  • Compliance records: drug/alcohol screening results with signatures, incident reports, and grievances.
  • Signed documents (e.g. house agreements) captured as images.

How it's used

Solely to operate the residence — tracking recovery progress, accountability, and the records a residence needs for its own compliance. We do not sell personal information or use it for advertising.

Who can see it

A resident's records are visible to the staff and owner of their residence. Residents see their own information. Access is enforced by database security rules so that signed-in users only reach the records they're permitted to.

How it's protected

  • Encrypted in transit (HTTPS) and at rest by our database provider.
  • Access requires a login that issues a time-limited token; data tables are not open to the public.
  • Encrypted backups are kept to prevent data loss.

Service providers

We rely on Supabase (database/authentication), Cloudflare (hosting/delivery), and Resend (delivery of opt-in reminder emails). These providers process data only to deliver the service.

Retention

Records are kept while a residence uses InStep and for as long as needed for the residence's compliance and recordkeeping. Discharged residents' records are retained rather than deleted, to preserve the residence's history, unless an operator requests removal.

Your choices

Residents can ask their residence's staff to review, correct, or remove their information. Operators control their data and can request export or deletion.
