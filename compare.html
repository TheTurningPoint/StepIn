What you get with InStep

  • Live house dashboard — who's in and who's out, right now
  • GPS-verified meeting check-ins with witness signatures — real accountability, not the honor system
  • Curfew sign in/out, chores, events, and announcements
  • Drug-test logs, incident reports, and a resident grievance loop (the resident-voice piece NARR looks for)
  • Signed documents and printable compliance reports
  • A resident app residents actually open every day
  • Your own branded web address — yourhouse.instepapp.com

Where the bigger platforms differ

  • One Step is built for licensed treatment centers — medical records, e-prescribing, medical billing, scheduling. Powerful, but it's a clinical system; for a sober-living house most of it is weight you'll never use.
  • Sobriety Hub is closer in scope and has built-in rent payments today. InStep handles rent through a simple payment link for now, with in-app billing on the roadmap.

The difference that matters

InStep is built by someone with lived recovery experience — designed around the NARR Standard, for the way a house really works. That's why the parts residents touch every day are the strong parts: check-ins people actually complete, real meeting accountability, and a way for residents to be heard.

It's simple enough that a busy manager can use it on a phone in seconds — and when you need something, you reach a person, not a ticket queue.
